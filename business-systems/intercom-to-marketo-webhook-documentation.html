SECTION 1: EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

1. Executive Summary

Intercom to Marketo Contact Subscribed Webhook is a Workato integration recipe that listens for contact resubscription events from Intercom's production workspace (App 6) via an HTTP webhook, then synchronizes the subscription status to Marketo by setting the lead's unsubscribed field to false. This ensures that when a contact opts back into email communications within Intercom, the preference change is propagated across the entire marketing technology stack -- Intercom, Marketo, Salesforce, and Outreach.

The recipe implements a robust decision tree with four distinct outcome paths: successful resubscription, lead not found in Marketo, lead already subscribed, and Marketo update failure. Each outcome triggers an email notification to workato-unsubscribe@intercom.io and publishes an event to the centralized AOF (Automation Observability Framework) topic for monitoring and audit.

Key Metrics: 1,264 successful runs, 7 failures (99.4% success rate). Active since 2024-05-22. Last executed 2026-04-15. Processing ~2.8 resubscriptions per day on average.

SECTION 2: BUSINESS OVERVIEW

2. Business Overview

Business Purpose

When a contact in Intercom changes their email subscription preference from "unsubscribed" to "subscribed" (i.e., the unsubscribed_from_emails field changes from TRUE to FALSE), this recipe ensures the change is immediately reflected in Marketo. Without this integration, a contact who resubscribes in Intercom would continue to be suppressed from marketing emails in Marketo, Salesforce, and Outreach -- leading to missed engagement opportunities and a poor customer experience.

Business Context

This recipe is part of the Subscription Preference Synchronization framework, which maintains consistency of email opt-in/opt-out status across the four core systems in Intercom's marketing and sales stack. The companion recipe handles the reverse flow (unsubscription events). Together, they ensure that consent preferences are always in sync regardless of which system the contact uses to manage their preferences.

Stakeholders

  • Marketing Operations -- Relies on accurate subscription status in Marketo for campaign targeting and compliance
  • Sales Operations -- Needs correct opt-in status flowing through to Salesforce and Outreach for outbound sequences
  • Legal / Compliance -- Email consent must be honored across all systems to meet GDPR, CAN-SPAM, and CCPA requirements
  • Customer Support -- Uses Intercom as the primary interface where contacts manage their preferences

Business Impact

Failure in this recipe means contacts who want to receive emails would remain suppressed in marketing and sales systems, directly impacting lead nurture conversion rates, sales outreach effectiveness, and customer satisfaction. The recipe processes approximately 2-3 resubscription events per day with a 99.4% success rate.

SECTION 4: ARCHITECTURE OVERVIEW

4. Architecture Overview

The recipe follows a webhook-triggered, event-driven architecture. Intercom fires a webhook whenever a contact resubscribes to emails. Workato receives the webhook, enriches the data by searching for the matching lead in Marketo, then conditionally updates the lead's subscription status. The architecture employs a multi-branch decision tree to handle four distinct scenarios, with comprehensive notification and observability at every branch.

Key Architectural Patterns

  • Event-Driven Trigger: HTTP webhook provides real-time, push-based integration (no polling delay)
  • Lookup-Before-Update: Searches Marketo for matching lead by email before attempting update, preventing blind writes
  • Defensive Branching: Four explicit outcome paths with notifications ensure no event is silently dropped
  • Try/Catch with Retry: Marketo update wrapped in error handling with 3 retries at 10-second intervals
  • Centralized Observability: Every outcome publishes to AOF Pub/Sub topic for monitoring dashboards

SECTION 6: END-TO-END FLOW

6. End-to-End Flow

Flow Narrative

The integration begins when a contact in Intercom (App 6) changes their email subscription preference from "unsubscribed" to "subscribed." Intercom fires a webhook to Workato containing the full contact payload, including the contact's email, name, workspace ID, and custom attributes. The recipe then executes the following sequence:

  1. Receive Webhook: Workato receives the JSON payload from Intercom's contact subscribed event
  2. Search Marketo: Using the contact's email address from the webhook payload, search for a matching lead in Marketo (fields: email, unsubscribed, mktoName)
  3. Check Lead Exists: If zero leads are returned (or result is blank), send a "Lead Not Found" notification email, log the event, publish to AOF, and stop the recipe gracefully
  4. Iterate Leads: For each matching lead found, accumulate into a Records list and evaluate whether the lead is already subscribed
  5. Check Already Subscribed: If the lead's unsubscribed field is already false (not true), send an "Already Subscribed" notification and log it
  6. Update Lead: If the lead is currently unsubscribed, attempt to update the lead in Marketo by setting unsubscribed = false, wrapped in a Try/Catch block
  7. Handle Success: On successful update, send a "Success" notification email, publish SUCCESS to AOF, and log the outcome
  8. Handle Failure: On failure (after 3 retries at 10s intervals), send a "Failed" notification email, publish ERROR to AOF, and stop the recipe with an error

SECTION 7: PROCESSING STAGES

7. Processing Stages

Stage 1: Webhook Reception (Trigger)

T
Receive Intercom Contact Subscribed Event
Workato HTTP webhook endpoint receives a JSON payload from Intercom when a contact's unsubscribed_from_emails attribute changes from TRUE to FALSE. The payload contains the full contact object nested at data.item.contact.

Stage 2: Marketo Lead Lookup (Step 1)

1
Search Leads in Marketo
Searches for leads in Marketo using the contact's email address as the filter. Retrieves fields: email, unsubscribed, mktoName. Filter type: email. Filter value: payload.data.item.contact.email.

Stage 3: Lead Existence Check (Step 2)

2
Conditional: Lead Found in Marketo?
Evaluates whether Marketo returned any leads. Condition: leads.size == 0 OR leads.size is blank. If TRUE (no leads found), proceeds to the "not found" branch. If FALSE (leads found), proceeds to the foreach loop.

Stage 3a: Lead Not Found Path (Steps 3-6)

3
Handle Missing Lead
Step 3: Send HTML email to workato-unsubscribe@intercom.io with subject: "[Name] requested to re-subscribe but a matching Marketo Lead record was not found"
Step 4: Log message to job report
Step 5: Publish LOGGING event to AOF Pub/Sub topic 37790
Step 6: Stop recipe (no error)

Stage 4: Lead Iteration (Steps 7-8)

7
For Each Matching Lead
Iterates over each lead returned from Marketo search. For each lead, accumulates a record into the "Records" list with fields: Marketo ID, Email, Full Name, Unsubscribed status. Uses simple repeat mode (sequential processing).

Stage 5: Subscription Status Check (Step 9)

9
Conditional: Already Subscribed?
Evaluates whether the lead's unsubscribed field is already false (condition: is_not_true). If the lead is already subscribed, sends an informational email and logs the event. If the lead is currently unsubscribed, proceeds to the update step.

Stage 5a: Already Subscribed Path (Steps 10-12)

10
Handle Already Subscribed
Step 10: Send email: "[Name] requested to re-subscribe but requestor is already subscribed"
Step 11: Log message to job report
Step 12: Publish LOGGING event to AOF Pub/Sub

Stage 6: Marketo Update (Steps 14-23)

14
Try: Update Lead in Marketo
Step 15 (Try): Update the lead in Marketo -- set unsubscribed = false using the lead's Marketo ID.
Step 16 (Catch): On failure, retries up to 3 times at 10-second intervals. If all retries fail, sends error notification (Step 17), logs (Step 18), publishes ERROR to AOF (Step 19), and stops with error (Step 20).
Steps 21-23 (Success): Sends success notification email, publishes SUCCESS to AOF, and logs the successful resubscription.

Stage 7: Completion (Step 24)

24
Stop Recipe (Graceful)
Recipe completes with stop_with_error = false. All outcome paths have been handled with appropriate notifications and observability events.

SECTION 11: EDGE CASES

11. Edge Cases

Edge Case 1: Lead Not Found in Marketo
If the Intercom contact does not have a corresponding lead record in Marketo (e.g., new contact not yet synced, email mismatch, or deleted lead), the recipe sends a notification email and stops gracefully. No update is attempted. The operations team must investigate and manually create or link the lead in Marketo.
Edge Case 2: Already Subscribed Lead
If the Marketo lead's unsubscribed field is already false, the recipe recognizes the redundancy, sends an informational notification, and skips the update. This prevents unnecessary API calls and ensures idempotency.
Edge Case 3: Marketo API Failure
If the Marketo update fails (e.g., API timeout, rate limiting, authentication error), the recipe retries up to 3 times at 10-second intervals. If all retries fail, an error notification is sent, an ERROR event is published to AOF, and the recipe stops with an error status. Manual intervention is required.
Edge Case 4: Multiple Marketo Leads for Same Email
The recipe iterates over ALL leads returned by the Marketo email search. If duplicates exist in Marketo (multiple leads with the same email), each lead is processed independently. This could result in multiple notification emails for a single webhook event.
Edge Case 5: Webhook Replay / Duplicate Events
If Intercom replays a webhook (retry on delivery failure), the recipe is effectively idempotent: if the lead is already subscribed, it is handled by the "already subscribed" path. If a concurrent execution occurs, the worst case is duplicate notification emails.
Edge Case 6: Contact Email is Blank or Invalid
If the webhook payload contains a blank or invalid email address, the Marketo search will return zero results, triggering the "Lead Not Found" path. The notification email will alert the team to investigate.
