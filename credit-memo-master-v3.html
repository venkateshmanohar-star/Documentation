Rec 5.0 — Credit Memo Master Recipe

Stripe Credit Notes to NetSuite Credit Memos — Automated Integration
ID: 54665263 Active Author: venkatesh.manohar Generated: 2026-04-01 833 successful runs | 0 failures

1. Executive Summary

What it does: This integration automatically creates Credit Memo records in NetSuite whenever a credit note is issued in Stripe. It reads credit note line items from a Snowflake staging table, groups them by credit note, ensures the customer exists in NetSuite, checks for Customer Balance Adjustments (CBAs), and writes the Credit Memo with all appropriate line items, discounts, and revenue recognition dates.

Why it exists: When Intercom issues a refund, cancellation credit, or billing adjustment in Stripe, the finance team needs a corresponding Credit Memo in NetSuite for accurate revenue reporting, reconciliation, and audit compliance. Without automation, finance would need to manually create hundreds of Credit Memos per month.

High-level flow: Stripe credit notes flow through Fivetran/dbt into a Snowflake staging table. Workato reads unprocessed rows, groups line items by credit note, validates or creates the customer in NetSuite, determines whether a CBA (Customer Balance Adjustment) applies, and then creates the Credit Memo record. Every action is logged back to Snowflake for deduplication and auditability.

2. Business Overview

Problem Statement

When a customer receives a credit in Stripe (due to cancellation, downgrade, billing error, or contractual adjustment), the finance system (NetSuite) must reflect this credit as a Credit Memo. This document is required for:

  • Revenue recognition: Credits reduce recognized revenue in the correct period
  • Reconciliation: Finance must tie Stripe credits back to NetSuite transactions
  • Audit trail: Every financial adjustment needs a corresponding record in the ERP
  • Customer balance accuracy: Credit balances on customer accounts must be accurate

What Happens from a Business Perspective

Step 1: A credit note is created in Stripe (e.g., customer cancels mid-cycle, receives proration credit).

Step 2: The credit note data flows into Snowflake via Fivetran and is transformed by dbt into the staging table NETSUITE_EXPORT_CREDIT_MEMO_CREDIT_NOTES.

Step 3: This recipe picks up unprocessed credit note line items, groups them, and creates a Credit Memo in NetSuite with the correct customer, line items, amounts, discounts, currency, and revenue recognition dates.

Step 4: If a Customer Balance Adjustment exists in Stripe, the recipe also applies it to the Credit Memo in NetSuite.

4. Architecture Overview

Source Layer (Stripe)

Business Role

Stripe is the billing system where credit notes originate. When a customer receives a credit (cancellation, refund, adjustment), Stripe creates a credit note object.

Technical Behavior

Stripe credit notes are extracted by Fivetran into FIVETRAN.FIVETRAN_STRIPE. dbt models join credit note lines with invoice data, customer data, and contract data to produce the staging table.

Data Layer (Snowflake)

Business Role

Snowflake holds the prepared data: credit note line items ready to become NetSuite Credit Memos, customer records for validation, and logs tracking what has already been processed.

Technical Behavior

Key tables in SHARED_EXTERNAL_WORKATO schema: the export table (3,724 rows) provides credit memo line items; WORKATO_LOGS tracks successful writes for deduplication; NETSUITE_EXPORT_CUSTOMER and NETSUITE_EXPORT_CUSTOMER_CREDIT_ADJUSTMENTS provide customer and CBA lookup data.

Orchestration Layer (Workato)

Business Role

Workato is the automation engine that reads from Snowflake and writes to NetSuite. It handles the business logic: grouping line items, validating customers, handling discounts and CBAs.

Technical Behavior

Three recipes form a hierarchy. The master recipe (54665263) queries Snowflake and iterates over credit note groups. For each group, it calls RF 5.0 (54665273) which handles customer validation, CBA lookup, and Credit Memo creation. RF 5.0 may call Rec 1.01 (54665259) to create/update the customer in NetSuite if not found.

Target Layer (NetSuite)

Business Role

NetSuite is the system of record for financial transactions. Credit Memos created here reduce recognized revenue, update customer balances, and are available for reconciliation and reporting.

Technical Behavior

NetSuite receives Credit Memo records via SuiteTalk API. Each Credit Memo includes: customer reference, line items with amounts, revenue recognition dates, currency/exchange rate, and optional CBA application. The customer record is upserted by external ID (Stripe customer ID).

6. End-to-End Flow

Level 0 — Simple View

A credit note is created in Stripe → data lands in Snowflake → Workato reads it, groups line items by credit note → for each credit note, Workato ensures the customer exists in NetSuite → Workato creates the Credit Memo in NetSuite with all line items, discounts, and CBA adjustments → result is logged for deduplication.

Level 1 — Detailed View

  1. Extraction: Rec 5.0 queries NETSUITE_EXPORT_CREDIT_MEMO_CREDIT_NOTES joined with WORKATO_LOGS to find rows where amount != 0 and no successful prior write exists (deduplication). Filtered by start date parameter and up to yesterday.
  2. Grouping: Line items are collected into a list. When the STRIPE_CREDIT_NOTE_ID changes from one row to the next, the accumulated batch is sent to RF 5.0 for processing, and a new batch begins.
  3. Customer Validation (RF 5.0): RF 5.0 searches NetSuite for the customer by external ID. If not found, it calls Rec 1.01 to create/update the customer using data from NETSUITE_EXPORT_CUSTOMER.

  7. Logging: Success or failure is logged via the mapper action. The log is used by the next run for deduplication.

7. Processing Stages

Stage 1: Ingestion

What Happens

The recipe reads all credit note line items from Snowflake that have not yet been processed. It filters out zero-amount rows and rows that were already successfully written to NetSuite.

How It Works

A SQL query joins NETSUITE_EXPORT_CREDIT_MEMO_CREDIT_NOTES with WORKATO_LOGS using a LEFT JOIN on stripe_credit_note_id = external_id. Rows where wl.netsuite_internal_id IS NULL (no prior success log) and amount != 0 are selected. Date range: from the start date parameter to yesterday.

Stage 2: Grouping

What Happens

Line items belonging to the same credit note are grouped together. A single credit note may have multiple line items (e.g., subscription line + proration line + discount line).

How It Works

The master recipe iterates over rows ordered by created_date ASC. It accumulates rows into a list variable. When the STRIPE_CREDIT_NOTE_ID changes, the current batch is sent to RF 5.0 via call_recipe, and the list is cleared for the next group.

Stage 3: Customer Validation

What Happens

Before creating a Credit Memo, the system ensures the customer exists in NetSuite. If the customer is missing, it creates one automatically using Stripe customer data from Snowflake.

How It Works

RF 5.0 calls netsuite.search_object_v2 to search for the customer by external ID. If not found, it calls Rec 1.01 (ID: 54665259) which queries NETSUITE_EXPORT_CUSTOMER in Snowflake and calls RF 1.0 (ID: 54251831) to create/update the customer in NetSuite via SuiteTalk.

Stage 4: CBA & Sales Order Lookup

What Happens

The system checks whether the credit note has an associated Customer Balance Adjustment (a credit applied to the customer's account balance rather than refunded). It also looks up the related Sales Order in NetSuite.

How It Works

RF 5.0 queries NETSUITE_EXPORT_CUSTOMER_CREDIT_ADJUSTMENTS where type = 'credit_note' and credit_note matches the current Stripe credit note ID. It also searches NetSuite for the Sales Order via search_object_v2. Custom Ruby code processes the CBA data to determine the correct amount and NS internal ID.

Stage 5: Credit Memo Creation

What Happens

The Credit Memo is created in NetSuite with all line items, the correct customer, currency, exchange rate, dates, and optional CBA and discount lines.

How It Works

RF 5.0 builds the Credit Memo payload with: entity (customer NS ID), tranDate, currency, exchangeRate, line items (each with item, quantity, amount, revRecStartDate, revRecEndDate), and optional discount/CBA lines. It calls netsuite.add_object to create the record. Two paths exist depending on whether a CBA is present, each producing a slightly different payload.

Stage 6: Logging

What Happens

Every outcome (success or failure) is recorded so the system knows not to reprocess the same credit note.

How It Works

The workato_mapper.map action constructs a log entry that is ultimately written to the WORKATO_LOGS table in Snowflake. On success, the netsuite_internal_id of the created Credit Memo is recorded. On failure (catch block), the error type and message are logged instead.

9. Key Business Logic

Credit Note Grouping

Credit notes in Stripe can have multiple line items (e.g., one for the subscription charge, one for a proration, one for a discount). The recipe groups all line items sharing the same STRIPE_CREDIT_NOTE_ID into a single batch before creating one Credit Memo in NetSuite.

Customer Balance Adjustment (CBA) Handling

What is a CBA? When Stripe issues a credit note, the credit can either be refunded to the payment method or applied to the customer's account balance. The latter creates a Customer Balance Adjustment (CBA). In NetSuite, CBAs need to be represented as a separate line on the Credit Memo.

Logic: RF 5.0 queries the NETSUITE_EXPORT_CUSTOMER_CREDIT_ADJUSTMENTS table for records where type = 'credit_note' and the credit note ID matches. If found, the CBA amount, NS internal ID, and subsidiary are extracted. The Credit Memo is then created with an additional CBA line.

Customer Creation / Upsert

If the customer referenced by the credit note does not exist in NetSuite, the recipe automatically creates one. Rec 1.01 queries NETSUITE_EXPORT_CUSTOMER in Snowflake for the full customer record (name, billing address, subsidiary, currency, Salesforce ID, terms, email) and passes all fields to RF 1.0 which performs the actual NetSuite upsert using the Stripe customer ID as the external ID.

Discount Handling

When a credit note line has a non-zero DISCOUNT_AMOUNT, the recipe adds a discount line item to the Credit Memo. The discount percentage and discount item code are pulled from the staging table fields DISCOUNT_PERCENTAGE and DISCOUNT_ITEM.

Revenue Recognition Dates

Each Credit Memo line item carries REV_REC_START_DATE and REV_REC_END_DATE from the staging table. These dates flow through to NetSuite to ensure revenue is recognized (or de-recognized) in the correct accounting period.

Collection Method Branching

The COLLECTION_METHOD field (values: send_invoice or charge_automatically) influences how the Credit Memo is mapped. This distinguishes sales-led invoices (sent manually) from self-serve automatic charges.

Why Grouping is Needed

The staging table is at the line item grain — one row per credit note line item. A single Stripe credit note (e.g., cn_xxx) may have 1 to N line items. NetSuite expects a single Credit Memo record with multiple sub-lines. Therefore, the recipe must group line items by STRIPE_CREDIT_NOTE_ID before creating the Credit Memo.

11. Edge Cases & Special Scenarios

Missing Customer in NetSuite

Scenario: The credit note references a Stripe customer that does not yet exist in NetSuite.

Handling: RF 5.0 detects this via the NetSuite search returning no results. It automatically calls Rec 1.01 → RF 1.0 to create the customer using data from the NETSUITE_EXPORT_CUSTOMER Snowflake table. Processing then continues normally.

Risk: If the customer data is also missing from Snowflake, the customer creation will fail. This is rare since credit notes are always linked to existing Stripe customers.

Missing Sales Order

Scenario: No matching Sales Order is found in NetSuite for the credit note.

Handling: The recipe continues and creates the Credit Memo without a Sales Order link. The CM is still valid but may require manual linking by the finance team.

Zero-Amount Lines

Scenario: A credit note line item has an amount of zero.

Handling: Filtered out at the SQL level (amount != 0). These lines are never sent to NetSuite.

CBA Without NetSuite Internal ID

Scenario: A CBA record exists in Snowflake but has no netsuite_internal_id (meaning the CBA has not yet been synced to NetSuite).

Handling: The custom Ruby code in RF 5.0 processes this case. If the CBA NS internal ID is null, the Credit Memo is created without the CBA line, and the CBA will need to be applied separately once synced.

Negative Amounts / Reversals

Scenario: Credit note line items are inherently negative (they reduce amounts owed).

Handling: The AMOUNT field in the staging table is already signed appropriately. The recipe passes amounts directly to NetSuite, which interprets them correctly on a Credit Memo.

Duplicate Prevention

Scenario: The recipe runs again and picks up the same credit note.

Handling: The SQL query joins with WORKATO_LOGS and only selects rows where no successful write exists (wl.netsuite_internal_id IS NULL). This ensures already-processed credit notes are skipped.

12. Operational Notes

Deduplication Mechanism

The deduplication strategy uses the WORKATO_LOGS table. After each successful Credit Memo creation, a log entry is written with:

  • external_id = STRIPE_CREDIT_NOTE_ID
  • source_table_name = 'SHARED_EXTERNAL_WORKATO.NETSUITE_EXPORT_CREDIT_MEMO_CREDIT_NOTES'
  • environment_type = 'prod'
  • is_successful_write_to_netsuite = true
  • netsuite_internal_id = the ID of the created Credit Memo

The next run's SQL query uses a LEFT JOIN with WORKATO_LOGS and filters for wl.netsuite_internal_id IS NULL, effectively skipping any credit note that was already processed. The log uses QUALIFY ROW_NUMBER() to deduplicate multiple log entries for the same external ID, keeping only the most recent.

Error Handling

Both the master recipe (Rec 5.0) and the child recipe (RF 5.0) use try/catch blocks:

  • Rec 5.0 catch block: Catches errors during the overall iteration. If the entire batch fails, the error is logged.
  • RF 5.0 catch block: Catches errors during individual credit memo creation. The error type and message are mapped and logged. This means a failure on one credit note does not stop processing of subsequent credit notes.

The zero failure count (0 out of 833 runs for Rec 5.0, 0 out of 2,819 runs for RF 5.0) indicates robust error handling at the individual record level.

Limitations & Considerations

  • Sequential processing: Credit notes are processed one group at a time within the foreach loop. High volumes may take longer.
  • CBA dependency: If a CBA has not been synced to NetSuite yet (no netsuite_internal_id in the CBA table), the Credit Memo will be created without the CBA line. Manual reconciliation may be needed.
  • Manual invoices: CBAs originating from manual invoices (proration/downgrade) may not be present in NetSuite if the routing debug string in the NS ID prevents RF 8.0 from parsing.
  • Date parameter: The recipe requires a Start_Date parameter to set the lower bound for processing. This is typically set by the calling scheduler.
  • Ordering: Rows are processed in created_date ASC order, ensuring older credit notes are handled first.
