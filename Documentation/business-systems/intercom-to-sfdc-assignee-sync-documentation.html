Intercom to SFDC Conversation Assignee Sync

Workato Recipe ID: 15918702 | Automated CRM ownership alignment via Intercom conversation tags
Author: venkatesh.manohar Generated: 2026-04-15 Status: Active Folder ID: 2817977
SECTION 1

1. Executive Summary

This Workato recipe synchronizes Intercom conversation assignee ownership to Salesforce Lead and Contact records. When a conversation in Intercom's App 6 workspace is tagged with "Sync Convo Owner to SFDC", the recipe automatically identifies the conversation's assigned agent, locates the corresponding Contact or Lead in Salesforce, and updates the record owner to match the conversation assignee -- subject to role-based SLA windows and Outreach sequence safeguards.

SECTION 2

2. Business Overview

Business Problem

When sales and support reps engage with customers via Intercom conversations, the Salesforce CRM ownership of the corresponding Lead or Contact may not reflect the person actually working the relationship. This misalignment causes:

  • Inaccurate pipeline attribution and forecasting
  • Missed follow-ups when the CRM owner is not the active conversation agent
  • Routing conflicts between Outreach sequences and manual Intercom interactions
  • Loss of sales activity context when ownership is outdated

Business Solution

This integration bridges the gap between real-time customer engagement in Intercom and CRM record ownership in Salesforce. By tagging a conversation with "Sync Convo Owner to SFDC", any team member can trigger an intelligent ownership update that respects SLA windows, active Outreach sequences, and role-based ownership rules.

4. Architecture Overview

The recipe follows an event-driven architecture triggered by Intercom webhook events. The flow operates as a sequential decision tree with parallel Contact and Lead processing paths. Key architectural patterns include:

  • Webhook-Triggered Execution: Intercom sends a tag event via HTTP webhook when a conversation is tagged with "Sync Convo Owner to SFDC"
  • API Enrichment: The recipe calls Intercom REST APIs to hydrate conversation, contact, and admin data not included in the webhook payload
  • Guard Rails: Multiple validation gates (unassigned check, email presence, Outreach sequence check) prevent invalid or conflicting updates
  • SLA-Based Ownership Rules: Role-specific time windows determine whether existing ownership should be respected or overridden
  • Error-Driven Loop Control: Uses try/catch with forced errors to break out of foreach loops after processing the first matching record
  • Observability: Every decision point publishes to the AOF PubSub topic for centralized monitoring

SECTION 6

6. End-to-End Flow

Flow Summary

  1. Trigger: Intercom webhook fires when a conversation in App 6 is tagged
  2. Filter: Only conversations tagged with "Sync Convo Owner to SFDC" (matched by intercom_tag_id account property) proceed
  3. Validate Assignment: If the conversation is unassigned, publish to AOF and stop
  4. Enrich Data: Fetch full conversation, contact (email), and admin (assignee email) from Intercom REST API
  5. Validate Emails: If contact email or admin email is missing, stop with appropriate logging
  6. Match SFDC User: Search Salesforce for a User matching the conversation assignee's email
  7. Wait: Pause 15 minutes for SFDC Lead/Contact record creation to complete
  8. Search SFDC Records: Search for matching Contact and Lead records by the contact's email
  9. Outreach Guard: If the Contact/Lead is in an active Outreach sequence, skip ownership update
  10. SLA Check: Determine the role-based SLA window (14/30/60 days) and check recent Task activity
  11. Update Ownership: If rules allow, update the Contact or Lead owner to match the conversation assignee
  12. Log and Publish: Log outcome to job report and publish event to AOF PubSub topic

7. Processing Stages

Stage 1: Trigger and Validation (Steps 0-3)

0 Webhook Trigger

Receives Intercom webhook payload at /app6-conversations endpoint. Filters for events where payload.data.item.tag.id equals the intercom_tag_id account property (the "Sync Convo Owner to SFDC" tag).

1 Unassigned Check

If the conversation has no assignee, publishes an event to AOF PubSub and stops. There is no action to take if no agent owns the conversation.

Stage 2: Data Enrichment (Steps 4-13)

4 Get Conversation

Calls Intercom REST API GET /conversations/{id} to retrieve the full conversation model including all conversation parts, assignee details, and contact references.

5 Get Contact

Calls Intercom REST API GET /contacts/{id} to retrieve the contact's full profile, particularly their email address needed for SFDC matching.

6 Contact Email Check

If the contact has no email in Intercom, the recipe cannot match them in Salesforce. Publishes to AOF and stops.

9 Get Admin (Assignee)

Calls Intercom REST API GET /admins/{id} to retrieve the conversation assignee's admin profile and email address.

10 Admin Email Check

If the assignee admin has no email in Intercom, the recipe cannot match them to an SFDC User. Publishes to AOF and stops.

13 SFDC User Search

Searches Salesforce Users by the assignee's email to find the corresponding SFDC User record. If no match found, publishes to AOF and stops.

Stage 3: Wait and SFDC Record Discovery (Steps 17-22)

17 Wait 15 Minutes

Deliberate pause to allow time for Salesforce Lead/Contact records to be created by other integrations (e.g., Marketo-to-SFDC sync) before searching.

18 Search SFDC Contact

SOQL query searching Salesforce Contacts by the Intercom contact's email address.

19 Search SFDC Lead

SOQL query searching Salesforce Leads (un-converted only) by the Intercom contact's email address.

20 No Records Found Check

If neither Contact nor Lead records are found in SFDC, publishes to AOF and stops.

Stage 4: Contact Path - Ownership Resolution (Steps 24-107)

24 Contact Path Entry

If at least one matching Contact record exists, processes the Contact path first (Contacts take priority over Leads).

25 Outreach Prospect Search

Calls child recipe 39468878 to check if the Contact's email exists as an active Outreach prospect in an active sequence.

26-30 Outreach Sequence Guard

If the prospect is in an active Outreach sequence, logs the event and stops to avoid disrupting the active sales cadence.

31-107 Contact Ownership Resolution Loop

For each matching Contact, evaluates ownership rules: checks if owner already matches, if owner is non-human (Integration User/Queue), determines SLA window by role, checks recent Task activity, and either updates or preserves ownership.

Stage 5: Lead Path - Ownership Resolution (Steps 108-191)

108 Lead Path Entry

If no Contact records were found, falls back to processing matching Lead records using the same logic pattern.

109-191 Lead Ownership Resolution

Mirrors the Contact path logic: Outreach check, SLA determination, Task activity assessment, and conditional ownership update -- all applied to Lead records.

SECTION 9

9. Key Business Logic

9.1 Tag-Based Triggering

The recipe is not triggered by every Intercom conversation event. It only processes events where the tag ID matches the intercom_tag_id account property. This is the "Sync Convo Owner to SFDC" tag in production and a test tag in the development environment, allowing environment-specific behavior without code changes.

9.2 Contact Priority Over Lead

When both Contact and Lead records exist in SFDC for the same email, the recipe prioritizes Contacts (Step 24). The Lead path (Step 108) is only executed if zero Contact records are found. This prevents duplicate ownership updates and respects the typical Salesforce data model where Contacts represent converted, qualified relationships.

9.3 Outreach Sequence Guard

Before updating any SFDC record owner, the recipe checks whether the prospect is currently enrolled in an active Outreach sequence (Steps 25-30, 109-114). If active, the job stops immediately without making changes. This prevents disrupting automated sales cadences that depend on the current record owner.

9.4 Role-Based SLA Windows

Critical Business Rule: The SLA window determines the "staleness threshold" for existing ownership. If the current owner has recent SFDC Task activity within their SLA window, their ownership is preserved.
Owner RoleSLA Window (Days)Rationale
SDR I14 daysJunior SDRs have shorter ownership windows; stale records should be reassigned quickly
SDR II, AE, RM60 daysSenior sellers may have longer sales cycles; extended window protects active deals
Unassigned / Other30 days (default)Default window for records without a recognized sales role
Integration User / Sales QueueN/A (always update)Non-human owners are always overwritten with the conversation assignee

9.5 Task Assignee Matching Logic

When the existing owner's most recent Task is within the SLA window, the recipe performs an additional check: does the Task's assignee match the Intercom conversation assignee?

  • Task Assignee = Convo Assignee: Update ownership to the conversation assignee (both the most recent activity and the current conversation point to the same person)
  • Task Assignee != Convo Assignee: Do NOT update ownership. The ambiguity between two different active sellers means the recipe cannot safely determine the correct owner.

9.6 Unassigned Record Handling

If the SFDC Contact or Lead has no owner (unassigned), the recipe uses a simplified 30-day default SLA. If the record has recent Task activity within 30 days AND the Task assignee matches the conversation assignee, ownership is updated. If no recent activity exists, ownership is updated unconditionally since there is no active seller to protect.

9.7 Loop Control via Forced Errors

The recipe uses a design pattern where foreach loops are wrapped in try/catch blocks. After processing a record and updating (or deciding not to update) its ownership, a stop action with an error is triggered to break the loop. The catch block at Step 107 (Contact) and Step 191 (Lead) intentionally has no actions, silently absorbing the forced error and allowing the job to continue to the next phase.

9.8 Wait-Before-Search Pattern

Step 17 introduces a deliberate 15-minute delay between matching the SFDC User (assignee) and searching for the Contact/Lead record. This accounts for asynchronous record creation in Salesforce by other integrations (e.g., Marketo lead sync, form fills) and ensures the recipe searches for records that may still be in transit.

11. Edge Cases and Exception Handling

11.1 No Assignee (Unassigned Conversation)

Behavior: Job stops immediately at Step 1 with AOF notification. No SFDC changes are made.

Root Cause: Conversation was tagged before being assigned to an agent, or was auto-tagged by a rule.

11.2 Missing Contact Email in Intercom

Behavior: Job stops at Step 6. Cannot search SFDC without an email address.

Root Cause: Anonymous visitor, or contact was created without email information.

11.3 Missing Admin Email in Intercom

Behavior: Job stops at Step 10. Cannot map the assignee to an SFDC User.

Root Cause: Admin account configured without email, or a bot/automated assignee.

11.4 No Matching SFDC User for Assignee

Behavior: Job stops at Step 14. The conversation assignee has no corresponding Salesforce User.

Root Cause: The Intercom admin's email does not match any active Salesforce User.

11.5 No Contact or Lead Found in SFDC

Behavior: Job stops at Step 20 after the 15-minute wait.

Root Cause: Contact's email does not exist in SFDC even after the delay, or records have not synced yet.

11.6 Active Outreach Sequence

Behavior: Job stops at Step 30 (Contact) or Step 114 (Lead). Ownership is NOT changed.

Root Cause: The prospect is currently enrolled in an automated Outreach sales sequence; changing ownership could break the cadence.

11.7 Owner Already Matches Assignee

Behavior: Job stops at Step 65/68 (Contact) or Step 149/152 (Lead) without making changes.

Root Cause: The SFDC record owner already matches the conversation assignee -- no update needed.

11.8 Ambiguous Task Assignee

Behavior: If the most recent Task assignee differs from the conversation assignee and the Task is within the SLA window, ownership is preserved (no update).

Root Cause: Two different sellers are actively working the same record. The recipe cannot determine the "correct" owner and errs on the side of caution.

11.9 SFDC Update Failures

Behavior: Wrapped in try/catch blocks. On failure, logs the error to the job report and publishes to AOF, then continues or stops gracefully.

Root Cause: Salesforce validation rules, insufficient permissions, record locks, or API limits.

11.10 Multiple Matching Records

Behavior: The foreach loop iterates through matching records but uses forced-error loop breaking to process only the first match.

Root Cause: Multiple Contacts or Leads share the same email address in SFDC.
